**Vs. Human SDR Teams:**

- Recommending AI replacement threatens internal sales team budgets and jobs

### Moat Type Definitions

For reference, the 8 moat types evaluated in this analysis:

**1. Speed (Organizational Agility)**
- Ability to iterate faster than competitors, adapt quickly to market changes, ship features rapidly
- Structural advantages enabling faster execution (team size, tech stack, lack of legacy constraints)

**2. Process Power (Operational Excellence)**
- Complex workflows with high reliability (99.9%+ uptime, quality standards, regulatory compliance)
- Processes that competitors cannot easily replicate due to difficulty or certification requirements

**3. Cornered Resources (Proprietary Assets)**
- Exclusive access to data, partnerships, talent, IP, or scarce resources competitors cannot obtain
- First-mover advantages in capturing unique datasets or forming exclusive relationships

**4. Switching Costs (Customer Lock-In)**
- Pain and effort required for customers to migrate to competitor (data migration, workflow integration, learning curve)
- Deep integration into customer workflows creating friction to switch

**5. Counter-Positioning (Incumbent Dilemma)**
- Business model forcing incumbents to harm existing business if they copy approach (Innovator's Dilemma)
- Structural reasons incumbents cannot follow without self-cannibalization

**6. Brand (Reputation & Trust)**
- Mental category ownership, reputation for quality/reliability, trust advantages taking years to build
- Brand-building mechanisms in model (virality, word-of-mouth, PR leverage)

**7. Network Effects (Value from Scale)**
- Product becomes more valuable as more users, data, or third-parties join
- Marketplace dynamics, social features, data network effects (direct or indirect)

**8. Scale Economies (Cost/Quality Advantage at Scale)**
- Unit costs decrease or quality improves significantly with scale
- High fixed costs amortizing at scale, supplier advantages, ML model improvements
