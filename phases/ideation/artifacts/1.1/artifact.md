## Interview Structure

### Section 1: Problem Discovery (Q1-Q8, 20 min)
**Focus:** Past behavior, not hypothetical. Probe for specifics and recency.

**Key Questions:**
- Q1: Last demo request process (baseline understanding)
- Q2: Last demo no-show (frequency validation)
- Q3: Lost deal due to delay (revenue impact)
- Q4: Pain severity 0-10 (quantified pain)
- Q5-6: Current workarounds (competitive landscape)
- Q7: Recent team conversation (active seeking)
- Q8: Magic wand fix (desired outcome)

**Target Outcomes:**
- 70%+ report pain â‰¥7/10
- Specific examples of recent pain
- Clear articulation of problem

---

### Section 2: Solution Exploration (Q9-Q16, 20 min)
**Focus:** Present solution neutrally. Listen for genuine resonance.

**Key Questions:**
- Q9: Present solution concept
- Q10: Immediate gut reaction (authentic response)
- Q11: Good/bad fit scenarios (use case validation)
- Q12: Trust requirements (must-haves)
- Q13: What would be replaced (displacement)
- Q14: Team/prospect concerns (objections)
- Q15: Outcome alignment (fit assessment)
- Q16: Decision-making process (buying process)

**Target Outcomes:**
- 60%+ positive resonance
- 50%+ of use cases fit
- Clear displaced spend identified

---

### Section 3: Willingness to Pay (Q17-Q27, 20 min)
**Focus:** Budget, pricing, and purchase intent.

**Key Questions:**
- Q17: Current budget allocation (baseline spend)
- Q18: Value in dollars (ROI framework)
- Q19: Last tool purchased (price anchoring)
- Q20: Preferred pricing model
- Q21-Q24: Van Westendorp Price Sensitivity Meter
  - Q21: Too Expensive (ceiling)
  - Q22: Too Cheap (floor)
  - Q23: Getting Expensive (upper optimal)
  - Q24: Bargain Price (lower optimal)
- Q25: Purchase intent 1-5 (commitment)
- Q26: Beta/pilot interest (early adopter)
- Q27: Anything else? (open-ended)

**Target Outcomes:**
- Clear optimal price range emerges
- 60%+ show purchase intent â‰¥4/5
- 3-5 early adopter candidates

## The Mom Test Principles

All materials follow Rob Fitzpatrick's "The Mom Test" methodology:

1. **Talk about their life, not your idea** - Focus on past behavior
2. **Ask about specifics in the past** - Avoid hypotheticals
3. **Listen more than you talk** - 70/30 listening/talking ratio
4. **Don't pitch** - Present solution neutrally, listen for resonance
5. **Dig into emotions** - Pain > Features
6. **Ask for commitments, not compliments** - Pilot interest, referrals
