## Target Personas

### Persona 1: VP Sales / Head of Sales (Mid-Market SaaS)
- **Segment**: Mid-Market (50-500 employees, $5M-$50M ARR)
- **Pain Focus**: Demo scheduling delays, no-show rates, sales rep utilization
- **Buying Power**: Primary budget owner
- **Recommended Interviews**: 5-6 (40% of total)

### Persona 2: Sales Operations Manager (Mid-Market/Enterprise)
- **Segment**: Mid-Market to Enterprise (200-2000 employees, $20M-$200M ARR)
- **Pain Focus**: Demo funnel metrics, tool stack costs, integration requirements
- **Buying Power**: Influencer (tracks data, identifies bottlenecks)
- **Recommended Interviews**: 3-4 (25% of total)

### Persona 3: Founder/CEO (Early-Stage SaaS)
- **Segment**: SMB (5-50 employees, $500K-$5M ARR)
- **Pain Focus**: Personal time spent on demos, scaling constraints, CAC
- **Buying Power**: Economic buyer (fast decision)
- **Recommended Interviews**: 2-3 (20% of total)

### Persona 4: Sales Enablement Director (Enterprise SaaS)
- **Segment**: Enterprise (500+ employees, $50M+ ARR)
- **Pain Focus**: Demo quality consistency, training costs, enterprise requirements
- **Buying Power**: Strategic buyer (large budgets, long cycles)
- **Recommended Interviews**: 2 (15% of total)

## Interview Script Structure

### Section 1: Problem Discovery (Q1-Q8, 20 min)
**Goal**: Validate that demo scheduling friction is a real, severe, active problem

**Key Questions**:
- Q1: Current sales process walkthrough
- Q2: Recent frustration with demo delays (recency test)
- Q3: Demo request volume and conversion rates
- Q4: Pain intensity score (0-10)
- Q5: Active workarounds (budget signal)
- Q6: Time spent on demos (opportunity cost)
- Q7: Demo personalization challenges
- Q8: Magic wand wish (top-of-mind pain)

**Maps to KPIs**: I1.1 (Pain Intensity), I1.2 (Workarounds), I1.3 (Recency)

---

### Section 2: Solution Exploration (Q9-Q16, 20 min)
**Goal**: Present solution concept and assess resonance without pitching

**Key Questions**:
- Q9: Initial reaction to AI demo agent concept
- Q10: Comparison to current tools
- Q11: Concerns and objections
- Q12: Use case scenario
- Q13: Quality bar and trust threshold
- Q14: Integration requirements
- Q15: Value if problem solved
- Q16: Likelihood to use (1-5 scale)

**Maps to KPIs**: I1.4 (Solution Resonance), I1.5 (Competitive Displacement), I1.6 (Outcome Alignment)

---

### Section 3: Willingness to Pay (Q17-Q27, 20 min)
**Goal**: Understand budget, current spend, and pricing expectations

**Key Questions**:
- Q17: Budget existence
- Q18: Current spend on demo tools/headcount
- Q19: Expected price (gut check)
- Q20: ROI evaluation criteria
- Q21-Q24: Van Westendorp pricing sequence
  - Q21: Too expensive (price ceiling)
  - Q22: Too cheap (price floor)
  - Q23: Expensive but acceptable
  - Q24: Bargain price
- Q25: Purchase intent at optimal price (1-5 scale)
- Q26: Pilot willingness (commitment test)
- Q27: Referrals (snowball sampling)

**Maps to KPIs**: I1.7 (Budget Existence), I1.8 (Purchase Intent), I1.10 (Van Westendorp Pricing)

## The Mom Test Methodology

All questions follow The Mom Test principles:

### DO:
‚úÖ Ask about specific past events ("tell me about the last time...")
‚úÖ Listen 70%, talk 30%
‚úÖ Ask follow-up questions ("why?" "tell me more")
‚úÖ Let silence happen

### DON'T:
‚ùå Pitch your solution (except neutrally in Section 2)
‚ùå Ask hypothetical questions ("would you use...?")
‚ùå Lead the witness ("don't you think this is a big problem?")
‚ùå Seek validation ("do you like my idea?")

### Red Flags:
- üö© Compliments without commitment ("that's a great idea!")
- üö© Hypothetical promises ("I would definitely use this")
- üö© Generic statements ("this is a problem for everyone")
- üö© Future tense ("we're planning to solve this next year")

### Green Flags:
- ‚úÖ Specific examples ("last Tuesday, we lost a $50K deal because...")
- ‚úÖ Emotional language (frustration, urgency)
- ‚úÖ Time/money spent ("I spend 10 hours/week on this")
- ‚úÖ Commitment ("if you build this, I'll pilot it next month")

### CONDITIONAL Verdict
- 5-7 KPIs pass thresholds

- Needs iteration on ICP or solution positioning

### NO-GO Verdict
- <5 KPIs pass thresholds
- Weak pain (<6/10)

1. **"Prospects won't talk to AI"**: Test quality bar and disclosure preferences
2. **"Too expensive"**: Test willingness to pay vs. current spend
3. **"Integration requirements"**: Test CRM/calendar/demo environment needs
4. **"Security/compliance"**: Test enterprise requirements (especially Persona 4)

### Expected Willingness to Pay
- **SMB (Persona 3)**: $200-$500/month
- **Mid-Market (Persona 1, 2)**: $500-$2,000/month
- **Enterprise (Persona 4)**: $2,000-$10,000/month

## Risks & Mitigations

### Risk: Low interview completion rate (<80%)
**Mitigation**: Over-recruit (schedule 15-20 to get 10-15 completions), send reminders 24 hours before

### Risk: Biased sample (only people who like AI)
**Mitigation**: Recruit from diverse channels, don't mention "AI" in outreach, ask about objections explicitly (Q11)

### Risk: Politeness bias ("that's a great idea!")
**Mitigation**: Follow The Mom Test rigorously, ask about past behavior (not hypotheticals), look for commitment (Q26)

### Risk: Inconsistent data collection
**Mitigation**: Complete tracking template immediately after each interview, use exact scoring scales (0-10, 1-5)
