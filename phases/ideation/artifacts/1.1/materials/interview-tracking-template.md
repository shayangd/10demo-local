## Quick Reference: Scoring Guidelines

### Pain Severity (0-10)
- **9-10:** Mission-critical problem, actively seeking solution now
- **7-8:** Major pain, high priority to solve
- **5-6:** Moderate pain, would consider solutions
- **3-4:** Minor annoyance, low priority
- **1-2:** Barely noticeable, not a real problem

### Purchase Intent (1-5)
- **5:** Definitely yes, have budget, ready to pilot/buy in <90 days
- **4:** Probably yes, need to see more but very interested
- **3:** Maybe, depends on price, features, timing
- **2:** Probably not, has concerns or barriers
- **1:** Definitely not, not interested or not a fit

### Van Westendorp Optimal Price Range
Calculate after ≥10 interviews:
- **Lower bound:** Where "Bargain" and "Getting Expensive" curves intersect
- **Upper bound:** Where "Too Cheap" and "Too Expensive" curves intersect
- **Optimal Point:** Where "Bargain" and "Too Expensive" curves intersect

## Interview Tracking Tips

**During Interview:**
- Record (with permission) so you can focus on listening
- Take notes on emotions and energy (excited, frustrated, skeptical)
- Mark questions where they gave especially strong responses

**Immediately After:**
- Complete this template within 1 hour while fresh
- Note 3-5 key quotes verbatim
- Flag for early adopter potential
- Send thank you and any promised follow-up

**Weekly Review:**
- After every 5 interviews, review aggregate patterns
- Adjust interview script if needed (add probes, skip irrelevant questions)
- Update persona distribution if one segment is much stronger
- Share emerging insights with team/advisors

**Data Quality Checks:**
- Van Westendorp prices should be logical (check logic box)
- Pain score should match qualitative description
- Purchase intent should match urgency and budget discussions
- If something feels off, note why and potentially re-contact
